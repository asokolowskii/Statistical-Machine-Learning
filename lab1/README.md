# Lab 1 – Monte Carlo Methods

## Overview

This project focuses on **Monte Carlo methods**, which use random sampling to approximate numerical quantities.

## Objectives

- Understand how randomness can be used for numerical estimation
- Implement simulation-based algorithms
- Analyze convergence and estimation error

## Implemented Experiments

### Estimation of π
Random points are generated inside a square, and the proportion of points inside the unit circle is used to approximate π.

### Volume Estimation in Higher Dimensions
Monte Carlo sampling is used to estimate the volume of a hypersphere relative to a hypercube.

### Random Direction Sampling
Vectors are generated and normalized to approximate random directions.

## Key Concepts

- Law of large numbers
- Convergence of estimators
- Sampling in high-dimensional spaces
- Bias and variance of estimates

## Conclusion

Monte Carlo methods provide a flexible approach to solving problems that are difficult to compute analytically, especially in higher dimensions.
